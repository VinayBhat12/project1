The R-squared score is a metric used to assess the goodness of fit of a regression model.
It quantifies the proportion of the variance in the target variable that is explained by the model.

#Model Performance
The R² score ranges between 0 and 1.

This indicates a strong correlation between the features and the target variable.

#Model Evaluation Metrics - R-squared and RMSE

#Model Performance Evaluation

Two key evaluation metrics have been calculated to assess the performance of the polynomial regression model.

#R-squared (R²) Score
R² measures how well the model explains the variance in the target variable.
In this case, R² = 0.93 (approximately), indicating a high degree of variance explained by the model.
A higher R² score suggests a better fit.

#Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE)
RMSE quantifies the average error between the predicted and actual values.
RMSE = 2.16 (approximately), which represents the average prediction error.
A lower RMSE indicates better prediction accuracy.

#Interpretation
R² of 0.93 suggests that the model captures approximately 93% of the variance in 'price,' indicating a strong fit.
RMSE of 2.16 means, on average, predictions are off by approximately 2.16 units of 'price.'

#Significance
High R² and low RMSE are indicative of a well-performing model.
These metrics provide insights into the model's accuracy and reliability.
